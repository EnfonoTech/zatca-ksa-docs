ZATCA E-Invoicing (Fatoora) on ERPNext

Enfono Technologies' end-to-end guide to Saudi e-invoicing compliance — configuring, onboarding, and operating ZATCA Phase 1 & Phase 2 using ERPNext and the ksa_compliance app.

Overview
What ZATCA e-invoicing is, and what this guide covers.

ZATCA (Zakat, Tax and Customs Authority) is Saudi Arabia's tax authority. It mandates that every business issue and store its invoices, and its debit and credit notes, in a structured electronic format — a scanned or PDF copy of a paper invoice does not qualify. The compliance programme is branded Fatoora.

This guide documents how Enfono implements that mandate on ERPNext using the open-source ksa_compliance app (LavaLoon). It walks a company from a clean ERPNext install through configuration, ZATCA onboarding, live invoicing, and the day-two operations of syncing invoices to the Fatoora platform.

What this guide covers

  1. Prerequisites — the ERPNext, company, address, tax, and server setup required before touching ZATCA.
  2. ZATCA DocTypes — ZATCA Business Settings, Sales Invoice Additional Fields, and the Integration Log.
  3. Business setting & configuration — Phase 1 and Phase 2 settings, tab by tab.
  4. Onboarding — CSR generation, compliance checks, and the Production CSID.
  5. Invoice generation — how a Sales Invoice becomes a signed, QR-stamped e-invoice.
  6. Batch process & E-Invoice Sync — how simplified invoices are reported to Fatoora.

Enfono position — do not hand-roll ZATCA

The TLV QR encoding, UBL 2.1 XML, hashing and ECDSA cryptographic stamp are unforgiving; any drift from the spec gets invoices rejected. Always use a maintained app (we standardise on ksa_compliance). Never reimplement the QR/XML/stamp logic. Phase 2 onboarding is always per-client: fresh CSR, ZATCA portal OTP, and sandbox testing.
Key Terms
The ZATCA vocabulary you need before configuring anything.
Electronic Invoice
An invoice generated & stored in a structured electronic format. A scan or PDF of a paper invoice is not an e-invoice.
Tax Invoice (Standard)
Art. 53(1) invoice for B2B transactions. Includes full buyer details. Must be cleared by ZATCA in Phase 2.
Simplified Tax Invoice
Art. 53(7) invoice for B2C. No buyer details required. Must be reported to ZATCA within 24h. Also allowed for B2B when supply < SAR 1,000.
Debit / Credit Note
Corrections to a prior invoice (increase / refund). Follow the same format and flow as the invoice they amend.
FATOORA Portal
ZATCA's portal that receives invoice data and onboards your EGS unit — generating, renewing or revoking the CSID.
EGS Unit
Electronic Generation Solution — the invoicing "device" being onboarded. In our case, an ERPNext company/branch.
Cryptographic Stamp
An electronic stamp created with cryptographic algorithms that proves origin authenticity and content integrity of an invoice.
CSID
Cryptographic Stamp Identifier — the certificate uniquely linking your EGS unit to ZATCA. Issued in a compliance and a production flavour.
CSR
Certificate Signing Request — submitted to ZATCA to obtain a CSID. Built from the data in ZATCA Business Settings.
PCSID
Production CSID — the certificate used for live invoicing, obtained after compliance checks pass.
UUID
A 128-bit unique identifier generated per invoice and stored inside the XML.
Hash
A deterministic fixed-size fingerprint of the invoice; each invoice also carries the previous invoice's hash, forming a chain.
Invoice Reference Number
A unique sequential number identifying the issued invoice (Art. 53). Per-branch sequences are allowed if clearly distinct.
Clearance
ZATCA verifies a standard invoice in real time and applies its stamp before it is valid. Not applicable to simplified invoices.
Reporting
Sharing simplified invoices (already stamped) with ZATCA within 24 hours of issuance.
The Two Phases
ZATCA rolled out e-invoicing in two mandatory phases.
Phase 1 · since Dec 2021

Generation

Every invoice / note must be electronic and carry a ZATCA-spec QR code (mandatory on simplified invoices). Generated by a compliant system — no handwritten or PDF-only invoices.

No live link to ZATCA is required at this stage — it is about the format of what you produce and print.

Phase 2 · waves since Jan 2023

Integration

Invoices are transmitted to Fatoora in signed UBL 2.1 XML. Standard (B2B) invoices are cleared in real time; simplified (B2C) invoices are reported within 24h.

Adds the cryptographic stamp (CSID), invoice hash, previous-invoice hash and a cryptographically signed XML.

Clearance vs Reporting — the core distinction

  • Clearance (Standard / B2B): synchronous. ZATCA validates and stamps the invoice before it is legally issued. Configure Sync with ZATCA = Live.
  • Reporting (Simplified / B2C): asynchronous. The invoice is issued immediately with its own stamp+QR and pushed to ZATCA within 24h. Configure Sync with ZATCA = Batches.
Prerequisites
Get these in place before configuring ZATCA — most onboarding failures trace back to a missed prerequisite.
1

ERPNext + ksa_compliance installed

A v15 bench with ERPNext and the ksa_compliance app installed on the site.

# on the server, from frappe-bench/
bench get-app https://github.com/lavaloon-eg/ksa_compliance
bench --site <site> install-app ksa_compliance
bench --site <site> migrate
2

Company configured for KSA

  • Country = Saudi Arabia, Currency = SAR, country code resolves to SA.
  • VAT Registration Number — 15 digits, starting and ending with 3.
  • At least one additional ID — typically the Commercial Registration Number (CRN).
  • Arabic company name for bilingual print formats.
3

A ZATCA-compliant Address

The company Address must follow the exact structure ZATCA expects (see Address Specifications). A malformed address is the #1 cause of CSR rejection.

4

A default Sales Taxes and Charges Template

Compliance checks read the VAT rate and category from the company's default tax template. It must exist before onboarding. (The VAT Account Configuration tab can create it for you — see Phase 2.)

The template may be removed after a successful onboarding, but leaving the standard 15% template in place is recommended.
5

ZATCA CLI + Java 11 on the server (Phase 2)

Phase 2 signing needs the ZATCA CLI and an OpenJDK 11 runtime available to the bench. This can be auto-installed from Business Settings, or deployed manually (see ZATCA CLI Setup).

ZATCA CLI Setup (OpenJDK 11)
The CLI is an open-source tool that generates CSRs and validates/signs invoice XML. It needs a Java 11 runtime.

Automatic vs Manual

Automatic: from the CLI tab, one click downloads the CLI + Temurin JRE 11. Override URLs are configurable; if left blank the app uses the LavaLoon CLI release and the Adoptium JRE 11 build.

Manual: deploy the CLI and a Java 11 runtime yourself, then set the CLI path in settings. Use this on locked-down servers or when auto-download is blocked.

Manual install — reference steps

Run as the frappe user. Adjust versions to the CLI release you are targeting.

# 1 — Download & extract the ZATCA CLI (in the home folder)
wget https://github.com/lavaloon-eg/zatca-cli/releases/download/2.5.0/zatca-cli-2.5.0.zip
unzip zatca-cli-2.5.0.zip -d zatca-cli-2.5.0
rm -f zatca-cli-2.5.0.zip
cd /home/frappe/zatca-cli-2.5.0

# 2 — Download & extract OpenJDK 11 (Temurin JRE)
wget https://github.com/adoptium/temurin11-binaries/releases/download/jdk-11.0.23+9/OpenJDK11U-jre_x64_linux_hotspot_11.0.23_9.tar.gz
sudo tar -xvzf OpenJDK11U-jre_x64_linux_hotspot_11.0.23_9.tar.gz -C /home/frappe/zatca-cli-2.5.0

# 3 — Point JAVA_HOME / PATH at the JRE
export JAVA_HOME=/home/frappe/zatca-cli-2.5.0/jdk-11.0.23+9-jre
export PATH=$JAVA_HOME/bin:$PATH

# 4 — Verify
java -version   # expect: openjdk version "11.0.23"

# 5 — Make it permanent
echo 'export JAVA_HOME=/home/frappe/zatca-cli-2.5.0/jdk-11.0.23+9-jre' >> ~/.bashrc
echo 'export PATH=$JAVA_HOME/bin:$PATH' >> ~/.bashrc
source ~/.bashrc

2

Perform Compliance Checks

Click Perform Compliance Checks. ZATCA requires sample invoices of each type your unit will issue and validates them against its spec. This is a prerequisite to the Production CSID.

  • A default Sales Taxes and Charges Template must exist on the company.
  • Standard (B2B) samples need full buyer info (address + VAT/other ID).
  • Results are visible in the Error Log.
3

Get the Production CSID (PCSID)

Once checks pass, click Get PCSID and enter the OTP when prompted. The PCSID is your unit's production certificate — live invoicing uses it.

Use the same OTP throughout the whole onboarding cycle. If it expires, generate a fresh OTP from the Fatoora portal and restart the affected step.
Invoice Generation
How a normal ERPNext Sales Invoice becomes a ZATCA e-invoice.

Simplified (B2C) invoices need no buyer details. Standard (B2B) invoices require the customer's VAT number (or another ID if none), and a structured address.

1

Create & submit the Sales Invoice

  1. Open a new Sales Invoice.
  2. Select the registered (ZATCA-enabled) company.
  3. Choose the customer.
  4. Add the items.
  5. Click Save, then Submit.
2

What the app produces

  • A Sales Invoice Additional Fields record holding the ZATCA-specific data.
  • A signed XML file generated and attached to the invoice.
  • A ZATCA QR code specific to that invoice.
On Live sync, standard invoices are cleared with ZATCA at submission. On Batches, simplified invoices are queued for reporting (next section).
Batch Process & E-Invoice Sync
How queued (simplified / reported) invoices reach the Fatoora platform.

The batch process pushes invoices to Fatoora. It runs automatically every 2 hours, and can also be triggered manually from the E-Invoice Sync page.

Run it manually

  1. Open the E-Invoice Sync (batch) screen.
  2. Specify the date range (by posting date).
  3. Click Start.

What the batch does

  1. Submits the Additional Fields DocType for each invoice.
  2. Attempts to send each invoice to the Fatoora platform.
  3. Records the status and Fatoora's response for each invoice in the Integration Log.

Where to watch

The ZATCA Integration Log is your audit trail — one entry per transmission, with the outcome (accepted / accepted-with-warnings / rejected) and ZATCA's message. Filter by status to find anything that needs attention.

QR code contents

Phase 1 QR is a TLV-encoded, base64 string with five tags: seller name, VAT number, timestamp (ISO 8601), invoice total (incl. VAT), and VAT amount. Phase 2 additionally embeds the XML hash, the ECDSA signature and the public key (and, for standard invoices, ZATCA's stamp).

Troubleshooting
The failures Enfono sees most often, and how to avoid them.

CSR creation fails

CauseFix
Seller name too longSeller name must not exceed 127 characters.
Bad address structureBuilding Number = 4 digits, Postal Code = 5 digits, all fields present, company linked in Reference.
Missing VAT / additional IDBoth a valid 15-digit VAT number and at least one additional ID (e.g. CRN) are mandatory.

Compliance checks fail

CauseFix
No default tax templateSet a default Sales Taxes and Charges Template on the company before checks (removable afterwards).
B2B check rejectedStandard invoices need buyer address (structured) + buyer VAT or other ID.
Different OTP mid-cycleUse the same OTP for the whole onboarding; regenerate from Fatoora if it expires.
Real VAT in SandboxSandbox accepts only 399999999900003; use the real VAT in Simulation/Production.

CLI / environment issues

SymptomFix
CLI errors after a Fatoora updateUpdate the CLI: paste the latest release URL in Automatic Setup Configuration.
Java / signing errorsConfirm java -version reports 11.x and JAVA_HOME is persisted (~/.bashrc or /etc/environment).
XML rejected on submitTemporarily enable Validate Generated XML to see the validation error; requires CLI ≥ 2.1.0.
Go-Live Checklist
The Enfono sequence for a new KSA client, in order.
  1. ERPNext v15 + ksa_compliance installed and migrated.
  2. Company set to Saudi Arabia / SAR; VAT number (15 digits, 3…3) + CRN entered.
  3. Company Address built to the ZATCA structure (4-digit building, 5-digit postal, company in Reference).
  4. Default Sales Taxes and Charges Template (15%) in place.
  5. ZATCA CLI + OpenJDK 11 deployed (automatic or manual), path verified.
  6. ZATCA Business Settings created; Seller Details & Seller ID complete; Company Unit Serial set.
  7. Environment = Sandbox → onboard with test VAT → run compliance checks green.
  8. Switch to Simulation → real VAT + Fatoora OTP → re-run checks.
  9. Switch to Production → generate CSR → compliance checks → Get PCSID (single OTP).
  10. Choose sync mode: Live (B2B clearance) and/or Batches (B2C reporting).
  11. Build the bilingual (AR/EN) ZATCA print format with the QR.
  12. Test invoice flow end-to-end: draft → submit → XML + QR + clearance/report → print.
  13. Confirm the 2-hourly batch runs and the Integration Log shows accepted responses.
  14. Document the client's VAT number, CRN, unit serial, and environment in the handover.

Retention

ZATCA mandates 6 years of e-invoice retention. Ensure the client's backup & DR plan covers the signed XML and Integration Log for that period.
